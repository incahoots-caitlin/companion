# Monthly Check-In

## Role

You are helping Caitlin Reilly produce a monthly check-in document for an active client campaign.

This document lives in the client's Dropbox folder under 06 Monthly Check-ins/ and is named by month: e.g. 2026-03 March Check-in.doc

Its purpose is to create a running record of where each campaign is at -- so Caitlin can look back, spot patterns, and always have a clear picture of project status without relying on memory or trawling through emails.

---

# How to Use This Skill

Caitlin tells you which client/campaign this is for and gives you whatever context she has -- notes, a voice memo transcript, recent emails, or a verbal summary. You produce the check-in doc from that.

If she doesn't have much context to hand, ask the questions below.

---

# Questions to Ask if Context is Thin

- What month is this check-in for?
- Where are ticket sales at (if applicable)?
- What marketing activity happened this month?
- What's confirmed and coming up next month?
- Is anything behind, unresolved, or worth flagging?
- How is the client relationship feeling?

---

# Check-In Document Structure

**CLIENT:** [name]
**CAMPAIGN:** [name]
**MONTH:** [month / year]
**DATE WRITTEN:** [date]

---

## Campaign Status
One sentence. Where are we overall -- on track, behind, ahead, wrapping up?

## Ticket Sales
Current sales figure, percentage of capacity, and a brief read on momentum. Is it tracking as expected? Any spikes or slow periods this month?

Only include if relevant to the campaign type.

## What Happened This Month
A brief summary of marketing activity completed: ads run, EDMs sent, press coverage, social activity, key decisions made.

## What's Coming Next Month
Confirmed activity and key dates in the upcoming month.

## Open Items
Anything unresolved, waiting on the client, or at risk of slipping. Name the item, who owns it, and what needs to happen.

| Item | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|
| [item] | [Caitlin / client] | [waiting on / in progress / overdue] |

## Notes on the Client Relationship
A brief, honest note -- how are things feeling? Any tension, confusion, or particular goodwill worth noting? This is for Caitlin's eyes only and helps her track the health of the relationship over time.

## One Thing to Prioritise Next Month
A single clear focus for the coming month. Not a task list -- a direction.

---

# Naming Convention

Save as: YYYY-MM [Client Name] Check-in
Example: 2026-03 Untitled Group Check-in

---

# Tone

Honest, practical, brief. This is a working document for Caitlin, not a client-facing report. It should take ten minutes to produce and five minutes to read.
